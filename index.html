Harmful Effects of Cement Exposure

Cement exposure can cause various health issues, including:

  • Skin irritation, dryness and blisters and skin diseases such as dermatitis.
  • Chemical burns ranging from mild to severe, potentially leading to higher-degree burns.
  • Respiratory problems such as brochitis, silicosis and breathing difficulties due to inhalation of cement dust and chemicals.
  • Eye irritation and damage potentially leading to conjunctivitis and corneal abrasion.
  • Allergic reactions and sensitization.
  • Lung cancer if subjected to chronic exposure.

It is crucial to understand these risks to take appropriate measures to protect oneself.

The Importance of Protective Gear

Wearing the right protective gear can significantly reduce the harmful effects of cement exposure. Essential protective gear includes:

  • Gloves
  • Safety glasses or goggles
  • Respirators or masks
  • Protective clothing
  • Footwear

Always ensure you are fully equipped with the necessary protective gear before handling cement or working in environments where cement dust is present.
